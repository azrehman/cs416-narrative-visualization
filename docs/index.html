Despite how connected the global economy is, stock exchanges based in different countries can experience remarkably different rates of growth and decline.

The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE), nicknamed "The Big Board")
is located in the Financial District of Lower Manhattan in New York City.
It is the largest stock exchange in the world.



The TSE runup from 1983 to 1990 was unprecedented,
in 1990 it accounted for over 60% of the world's stock market capitalization
before falling precipitously in value and rank to one of the 4th largest exchange in the world
by market capitalization of listed shares.
